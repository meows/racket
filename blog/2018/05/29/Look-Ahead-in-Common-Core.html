A look ahead in the CA Common Core for Math

Grade 5

Number Sense

  • Order of operations with addition, subtraction, multiplication, fraction, brackets.
  • Understanding the meaning of the digits in the base 10 system
  • Fluency with large whole numbers
    • quotients with up to 4-digit dividends and 2-digit divisors
  • Fluency with decimals up to hundredths

Rationals

  • Use the least common multiple to add and subtract fractions with different denominators. The least common multiple can be found via the greatest common divisor, but at this level children are likely expected to brute force the answer.
  • Multiply and divide rationals, and explain any steps. Interpret word problems as related to quotients, or apply quotients to situations.
    • "How much choclate will each person get if 3 people share 1/2 pounds of choclate equally?"
    • "How many 1/3 cup servings are in 2 cups of raisings?"
  • Fractions <=> Decimals
  • Plot a number line with fractions to demonstrate understanding of ordering.

Geometry

  • Area
    • area of square
    • area of triangle
  • What's volume?
    • formula for volume of cube and rectangular prism
    • familiarity with notation for unit square and unit cube for area and volume
    • volume can be added, subtracted, multiplied, and divided
  • Graphing a table of points with two dimensions
    • gain fluency over graphing a finite table of points
    • the axes are presented as 90 degrees ("orthogonal") to each other, and often labeled x for horizontal and y for vertical, and often listed in order of (x, y) when listed as a pair.

Grade 6

  • What's a ratio? How is it related to fractions?
    • Apply the notion of ratio to word problems: "There's a ratio of 2 boys to 3 girls in the room. If there are 6 boys, how many girls are there?"
  • Find the greatest common divisor (or greatest common factor) of two whole numbers less than 100.
  • Find the least common multiple of two whole numbers less than or equal to 12.
  • Fluency with negative numbers
  • Graph 2-dimensional tables of points which may now have negative numbers.

Variables

  • Use letters as abstract names for numbers in equations
  • Demonstrate fluency with variables by manipulating and simplifying expressions
  • Familiarity with the inequality operator
  • Area and volume of square and rectangular prism with variables
  • Area of triangle with variables

Statistics

  • mean
  • median
  • mode

Grade 7

Ratios, Proportionality, Lines, Graphs -- Tying it all together.

  • Understand that all proportional problems can be represented algebraically as y = mx.
